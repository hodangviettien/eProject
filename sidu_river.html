The Sidu River Bridge

Information
Finished

2009

Height

496m

Long

1.222m

Continent

Asia

Location

Hubei(China)


History

The bridge was designed by CCSHCC Second Highway Consultants Company, Limited. and built at a cost of 720 million yuan (approximately US$100 million). It opened to traffic on November 15, 2009.

Description

TStanding at an amazing 496 meters, the highest bridge in the world is the Sidu River Bridge in the Hubei province of central China. The Sidu River Bridge is a suspensions bridge that is 900 meters long that crosses a large gorge formed by the Sidu River as it meanders its way through the mountains.

When they first started building the Sidu River Bridge in 2005, the first thing the engineers did was shoot a rocket from one side of the gorge to the other which carried two pilot wires 980 meters across the huge gorge, how cool it that. It then took another 4 years to complete the Sidu River Bridge and it was then open to the public in 2009.

The bridge is a part of the G50 Huyu Expressway, a new highway that connects Shanghai to Chongqing. The suspension cables on the bridge are built from a whopping 127 strands of wire that are twisted into a hexagonal shape and these are what keep the bridge stable.
